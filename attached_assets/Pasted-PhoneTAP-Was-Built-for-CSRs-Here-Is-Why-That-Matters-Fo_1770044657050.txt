PhoneTAP Was Built for CSRs. Here Is Why That Matters.

For most of my career in HVAC, I misunderstood one of the most important roles in the company.

The customer service representative.

I Did Every Job. And I Got This One Wrong.

When I started my HVAC business, I did everything.
	•	Sales
	•	Service
	•	Installation
	•	Accounting
	•	Marketing
	•	Answering the phones

The role I underestimated the most was the CSR and dispatch function.

I thought it was easy.
I did not enjoy it.
So I hired for it early and trained for it poorly.

The training looked like this:
	•	Answer the phone
	•	This is what we do
	•	This is the service call price
	•	Put them on the schedule

No real sales training.
No objection handling.
No emotional intelligence.
No feedback loop.

I assumed they would just know how to do it.

That assumption was wrong.

CSRs Were Invisible in My Business

In my company, CSRs were not celebrated.

Sales had commissions.
Techs had performance metrics.
Installers had scorecards.

CSRs had booking rate and cancellation rate.
If those numbers were even accurate.

Without trustworthy data, I only noticed CSRs when something went wrong.
	•	Booking rate dropped
	•	A customer complained
	•	I overheard a bad call

My conclusion at the time was not “my systems are broken”.
It was “maybe I hired the wrong person”.

That was on me.

When AI Arrived, I Looked for the Wrong Solution First

When ChatGPT launched, my first thought was simple.

“AI voice can replace CSRs.”

Not because it was the right solution.
Because it was a way to avoid a role I never learned how to lead well.

At the time, I was running my company remotely. I needed visibility.

So I started by:
	•	Pulling call recordings from ServiceTitan
	•	Transcribing calls
	•	Summarizing them
	•	Dropping summaries into job records
	•	Scoring sentiment as good, neutral, or bad
	•	Sending alerts when calls went poorly

That alone helped save customers and escalate issues faster.

But it was not enough.

The Data Was Still Lying

I wanted to understand the call center the same way I understood:
	•	Sales metrics
	•	Service KPIs
	•	Install performance

So I went deeper.

The first thing I needed was accurate call classification:
	•	Booked
	•	Unbooked
	•	Abandoned
	•	Excused
	•	Not a lead

When I compared call transcripts to how calls were classified, I found a problem.

The data was wrong.
Not slightly wrong.
Very wrong.

I checked other companies I was working with.

Same issue.

Even companies manually reviewing calls had unreliable classifications.

Which meant leadership was making decisions based on bad data.

CSRs Are the Front Door to All Marketing Spend

This is where it clicked.

You can do everything right in marketing.
Spend thousands on ads.
Rank in search.
Build a strong brand.

And one phone call can erase all of it.

CSRs are not a back office function.
They are the gateway to revenue.

Why PhoneTAP Exists

I partnered with Josh Crouch and Tersh Blissett, combining:
	•	Years of operator experience
	•	Years of manual call review
	•	Deep marketing insight

That became PhoneTAP.

We trained AI using real calls reviewed by real humans.
Not theoretical prompts.

Today, we are confident in roughly 98 percent accuracy in call analysis.

But accuracy alone was not the goal.

I Did Not Want Another Dashboard That Only Management Likes

When we first built dashboards, they showed:
	•	Booking rate
	•	Unbooked rate

Then I asked a simple question.

“What can a CSR actually control here?”

A maintenance call is pre-sold.
A pricing question is not.

One unbooked service call can be far more expensive than five booked tune-ups.

So we changed the lens.

What CSRs Actually Do That Creates Value

PhoneTAP looks for things that were previously invisible:
	•	Objections raised by customers
	•	Objections successfully overcome
	•	Upset callers turned satisfied
	•	Emotional trajectory of each call
	•	High-stakes moments handled correctly

Example:

A customer asks about price.
That is an objection.

If handled poorly, the customer says “I will call back” and disappears.
If handled well, the customer books immediately.

Historically, there was no visibility into that win.

Now there is.

Celebrate What Is Going Right

PhoneTAP was not built as a surveillance tool.

It is not a “here is what you did wrong” system.

It is a recognition system.
	•	Objections overcome appear on the dashboard
	•	Upset customers calmed appear on the dashboard
	•	Positive momentum is visible
	•	Wins are measurable

Once value is visible, reward becomes possible.

Including performance pay.

Something CSRs have historically been excluded from because the data was unreliable.

Why AI Voice Will Not Replace CSRs Anytime Soon

Through deep analysis of real calls, I changed my mind.

Customers call in with elevated emotion.
They want to feel heard.
They know when they are talking to a machine.

If AI voice takes the easy calls, the remaining calls become harder.
Training gets worse, not better.

CSRs become even more important.

Built Bottom-Up, Not Top-Down

Most software is built for ownership.
Then forced downward for adoption.

PhoneTAP was built for CSRs.

When CSRs love a tool:
	•	Adoption is natural
	•	Managers see the value
	•	Ownership supports it

That is how real utilization happens.

PhoneTAP exists because I failed CSRs early in my career.
It exists to make their impact visible, measurable, and rewarded.

That was long overdue.
