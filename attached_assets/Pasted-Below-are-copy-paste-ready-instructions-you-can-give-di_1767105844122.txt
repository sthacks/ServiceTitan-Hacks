## Replit Instructions: January SmartAC Blog Post

### Goal

Write a January blog post for ServiceTitanHacks.com, sponsored by SmartAC, focused on why contractors overvalue new customers and undervalue existing customers, and why retention matters for 2026 planning.

The post should educate first, sell last. SmartAC should be positioned as a retention and engagement solution, not a lead source.

---

### Target Audience

* HVAC and home service contractors
* Owners, operators, and general managers
* ServiceTitan users
* Businesses doing $1M to $20M annually

Assume the reader understands basic marketing and operations concepts.

---

### Tone and Style

* Direct
* Practical
* Grounded in real contractor experience
* No hype
* No buzzwords
* No exaggerated claims
* No emojis

Write like an experienced contractor explaining reality to other contractors.

---

### Length

* 1,200 to 1,500 words
* Clear section headers
* Short paragraphs
* Skimmable formatting

---

### Blog Title

Use one of the following, or a close variation:

* "Why Contractors Overthink Attribution and Miss the Bigger Revenue Question"
* "Why HVAC Contractors Overvalue New Customers and Undervalue Retention"

---

### Required Structure and Content

#### Introduction

* Acknowledge that contractors care deeply about attribution and tracking revenue
* State that attribution is useful, but incomplete
* Introduce the idea that zooming in too far can hide more important strategic questions
* Set up the core theme: new customers vs existing customers

---

#### Section 1: Attribution Is Not the Same as Strategy

Cover:

* How contractors track lead sources, campaigns, and channels
* Why granular attribution feels productive
* Why attribution alone does not tell you how your business actually grows
* Emphasize that attribution is a tool, not the end goal

Do not criticize marketing agencies directly here. Keep it neutral.

---

#### Section 2: Why Contractors Overvalue New Customers (Part 1)

Explain:

* Most digital marketing agencies are built around new customer acquisition
* Common metrics agencies report:

  * Cost per lead
  * Cost per booked call
  * Cost per acquired customer
* What is rarely reported well:

  * Revenue from existing customers
  * Retention
  * Membership renewals
  * Long-term customer value

Make the point that this is structural, not malicious.
Explain how reporting shapes contractor behavior over time.

---

#### Section 3: Why Contractors Overvalue New Customers (Part 2)

Explain the psychological side:

* Describe the real-world scenario:

  * New customer calls
  * Same-day replacement
  * $20k to $30k contract
  * Money in the bank quickly
* Explain why this creates immediate gratification
* Contrast this with existing customer revenue:

  * Slower
  * Less dramatic
  * More predictable

Explain how this trains owners to chase short-term wins.

---

#### Section 4: The Cost of Ignoring Retention

Explain:

* What happens when churn is replaced with constant acquisition
* How this affects:

  * Marketing spend
  * Capacity planning
  * Technician utilization
  * Stress on operations
* Emphasize predictability vs volatility

No numbers are required. Focus on operational reality.

---

#### Section 5: The Bigger Year-End Question Contractors Should Ask

Introduce two questions:

1. What percentage of 2025 revenue came from existing customers vs new customers?
2. For a customer acquired in 2025, if they are retained, what revenue should be expected in 2026?

Explain why these questions matter more than individual channel performance when planning the next year.

---

#### Section 6: How to Think About Future Customer Value (Simple Model)

Explain conceptually, without math formulas:

* Membership revenue
* Ongoing service and repair revenue
* Replacement probability over time

Keep this high-level and easy to understand.

---

#### Section 7: Retention Is an Experience Problem

Explain:

* Customers leave when they forget you
* Silence between visits leads to disengagement
* Engagement builds trust and loyalty

No tech talk yet. Focus on customer experience.

---

#### Section 8: Where SmartAC Fits

Position SmartAC as:

* Not a lead source
* Not a sales script
* A customer engagement layer
* A system visibility and communication tool

Explain how SmartAC:

* Keeps customers connected year round
* Reduces reliance on tech-driven sales conversations
* Supports membership retention naturally

This should feel explanatory, not promotional.

---

#### Conclusion

* Reiterate that new customers matter, but retention compounds
* Encourage contractors to review:

  * Existing vs new revenue split
  * Membership retention
  * Engagement gaps
* Close with the idea that better retention creates a calmer, more predictable business
